Catalyst

In the beginning there was Bare Metal, and it was good.

BM is fast,

reliable*,

And it's all yours.

But suppliers were slow.

In time,

the people turned to VMs for scalability.

Providers began to allow self-service,

and so IaaS was born.

Self-service became

orchestration,

and IaC took the stage.

Containers shifted focus from platforms to applications,

giving us PaaS.

Then came serverless.

aka FaaS, NoOps, or "what the hell."

Of course there's a server…

Cloud is always just

"someone else's server."

IaaSYour OS, someone else's (virtual) hardware
 
PaaSYour application/container, someone else's OS
 
FaaSYour code, someone else's application

FaaS demo:

  • Customer's HR system provides updated list of users every night at 1am.
  • The list is available as a CSV file on their FTP server.
  • We need to import the list into Mahara.
